TOKYO
Here are some suggestions based on your preferences!
FOOD & DRINK Sushi lover? Wake up at 3am and go to the fish wholesale market for an unforgettable pre-breakfast experience, hanging out amongst both locals and fellow travellers. Watch the gritty tuna auction and watch fish traders agreeing deals long before the sun rises as the heartbeat of Tokyo’s food scene begins its day right here.
PLACES TO SEE Tokyo’s most visited temple, Senso-Ji, attracts a steady flow of worshippers who pass through clouds of incense to pay their respects. Passing through over 200m of bustling shopping street before you get to the temple’s entrance means you can stop for a snack before you prepare to spend several hours exploring one of Japan’s most colourful temples.
THINGS TO DO Shinjuku really doesn’t sleep. Hustle, bustle and noise is a 24/7 activity so just step right in and feel the intensity of Tokyo’s beating heart. Over 3 million people a day pass through the train station here so even just grabbing some street food and watching the world go by is an amazing way to get to know what really happens in this city.
